How to copy a Threads post link

On the Threads mobile app, open the post, tap the three-dot icon at the top right of the post, and choose Copy Link. On desktop, open the post on threads.net and copy the URL from your browser's address bar. Both formats work with ThreadSave — including threads.net, threads.com, and short /t/ share links.

What types of Threads posts can I download?

ThreadSave supports videos, single images, and multi-image carousels (also called sidecar posts). Mixed carousels containing both videos and images are handled automatically — each item is detected and downloaded at original quality. Text-only posts have no media to save and will return a friendly error.

Is it safe to use ThreadSave?

Yes. We don't ask you to log in, and we don't store your downloads on our servers. Files come directly from Meta's CDN — our server only acts as a stream proxy to bypass cross-origin restrictions your browser would otherwise enforce. See our Privacy Policy for details.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to the things people ask most.

How do I download a Threads video?

Open the Threads post, tap the three-dot menu, and copy the link. Paste the link into ThreadSave and click Get Media, then Download MP4.

Can I download multiple images from a Threads carousel?

Yes. ThreadSave detects carousel posts automatically and shows a Download All as ZIP button that bundles all images into a single ZIP file.

Is ThreadSave free?

Yes. ThreadSave is completely free. No account, no app, and no watermark on downloaded files. We're supported by non-intrusive ads on this page.

Does ThreadSave work on iPhone and Android?

Yes. ThreadSave works in any modern mobile browser including Safari on iPhone and Chrome on Android. On iOS, ZIP files open in the Files app where you can save them locally.

Can I download private or age-restricted Threads posts?

No. ThreadSave only works with public posts. Private or restricted posts are not accessible without logging in, and we don't ever ask you to log in.

Is there a download limit?

For abuse protection we cap each IP at 15 metadata fetches per minute and 10 file downloads per 10 minutes. Normal personal use will never hit these limits.
